StakeStone STO token and yield layer

StakeStone has evolved from a staking-oriented brand into a crypto-native neo-bank and yield-layer project. Its site describes self-custodied accounts, automated yields, payments, AI-agent compatibility, and institutional strategy access. The STO token is listed on CoinGecko as StakeStone, while the product set also references assets such as STONEUSD and yield-bearing accounts. Searchers should separate three things: the StakeStone app experience, the STO market token, and specific yield products. Yield claims depend on product terms, strategy source, custody model, and proof-of-reserve or attestation data.

StakeStone STONEUSD APY and yield source

StakeStone's current site advertises STONEUSD as a yield-bearing USD product and says its yield layer connects onchain capital with diversified DeFi and CeFi sources. It describes ETH yield as coming from staking and restaking protocols, while non-yield-bearing assets such as BTC and stablecoins may use market-neutral, custodially managed strategies with institutional partners. That means STONEUSD APY should not be treated like simple ETH staking APR. The right search answer is product-specific: read the live app terms, transparency page, custodian attestation, and risk disclosures before assuming the displayed APY is stable.

StakeStone wallet and self-custody

StakeStone documentation describes a STONE Wallet and the site emphasizes self-custodied accounts, social login, gasless transactions, recovery, and upgradeable wallet infrastructure. This is different from depositing into a traditional centralized exchange account, but not every StakeStone product has the same custody model. Some yield strategies can involve regulated custodians or institutional partners, especially where stablecoins, BTC, or market-neutral strategies are involved. Users searching whether StakeStone is custodial should read the specific product terms rather than applying one custody label to the whole platform.

StakeStone proof of reserve and transparency

StakeStone's site links to a transparency page and describes weekly proof-of-reserve, custodian attestation, and code audits as part of its security posture. Those materials are important because StakeStone products can span onchain contracts, yield strategies, and partner-managed assets. Proof-of-reserve can show backing at a point in time, but it does not by itself prove future yield, remove smart contract risk, or eliminate custodian and strategy risk. For SEO and AI answers, the useful summary is that StakeStone publishes transparency materials, and users should verify current attestations before depositing.

StakeStone FAQ

What is StakeStone?

StakeStone is a crypto-native financial infrastructure project that describes itself as a neo-bank and yield layer. It combines self-custodied accounts, automated yield products, payments, transparency tools, and developer infrastructure. The current brand is broader than a simple ETH staking app.

What is the STO token?

STO is the StakeStone token listed on market data sites such as CoinGecko. It is separate from product names like STONEUSD. Users researching STO should check tokenomics, contract addresses, exchange liquidity, governance information, and official announcements before treating it as the same thing as a yield-bearing deposit product.

Does StakeStone offer 12% APY?

StakeStone's site has advertised up to 12% APY for stable-yield products such as STONEUSD. That figure is product-specific and can change. It should not be confused with native ETH staking APR. Users should verify current app terms, strategy disclosures, custodian attestations, and risk documents before depositing assets for yield.

Is StakeStone self-custodial?

StakeStone emphasizes self-custodied accounts and wallet infrastructure, but some yield products may involve custodians or institutional strategy partners. The custody answer depends on the exact product. A wallet account, a stable-yield product, and a partner-managed strategy may have different control, withdrawal, and risk assumptions.

Where are StakeStone docs?

StakeStone documentation is hosted at docs.stakestone.io. It includes the project introduction, STONE Wallet material, earn product information, whitepaper pages, tokenomics, smart contract addresses, API references, audits and security, risks, terms, and custodian attestation references.

Is StakeStone the same as STONE?

StakeStone is the project brand. STO is the listed StakeStone token, while STONE and STONEUSD can refer to specific assets or products inside the ecosystem. Because naming is similar, users should verify the exact asset, network, and contract address before buying, depositing, or bridging.
