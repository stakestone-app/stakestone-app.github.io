How it works

StakeStone in three clear steps

The flow is built for wallet-native DeFi users: preview the asset path, sign from your wallet, and track the position on-chain instead of handing custody to a centralized account.

Step 01

Connect your wallet

Open the app and connect a compatible wallet. You keep the keys, review each transaction, and use the same wallet model common across Ethereum wallets.

Step 02

Preview ETH to STONE

Choose the ETH amount, review the STONE output, the route, the live rate, and the network cost shown inside the app before you approve anything.

Step 03

Confirm and manage

Sign the transaction from your wallet, then manage the STONE position through StakeStone's app, supported DeFi venues, or direct on-chain records.

Why StakeStone

Liquid staking infrastructure without giving up wallet control

Yield-bearing liquidity

STONE is designed as an ETH-backed yield-bearing asset for DeFi. It lets you keep exposure to staking-style returns while using liquid positions in compatible markets.

Built for omnichain use

StakeStone routes asset and price information across supported networks, using cross-chain infrastructure such as LayerZero messaging where the product supports it.

Clear before you sign

Rates, routes, timing, and costs can move with market and network conditions. The app shows live details before wallet confirmation so you can make the final call.

StakeStone focuses on STONE liquidity for Ethereum and supported EVM networks, with final availability always shown in the app.

FAQ

StakeStone staking questions

01

What is StakeStone?

StakeStone is a non-custodial DeFi app and yield infrastructure for STONE, an ETH-focused yield-bearing asset designed for liquid use across supported networks.

02

What asset do I receive?

The ETH staking flow previews STONE in the app. STONE is meant to represent ETH-based yield exposure while remaining usable in compatible DeFi markets.

03

How long does staking take?

Timing depends on wallet approval, network congestion, and the live route. The app shows the transaction state while on-chain settlement is pending.

04

What does it cost?

You pay network gas and any live route costs shown before confirmation. You can compare the on-chain fee environment with public tools such as the Ethereum gas tracker.

05

Which chains are supported?

StakeStone has focused on Ethereum and supported EVM networks for STONE liquidity. Check the supported chains page and the app for current availability.

06

Is StakeStone safe?

StakeStone is non-custodial, but DeFi still carries smart contract, market, asset, oracle, and cross-chain risk. Review the app details and broader DeFi market data before using it.
